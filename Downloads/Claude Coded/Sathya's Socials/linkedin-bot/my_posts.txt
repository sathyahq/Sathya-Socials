--- POST 1 ---
Recently, I & my 9-yr old daughter were watching the movie "Sookshmadarshini", starring Nazriya.

A character mentioned Alzheimer's.

She paused. Thought for a second. Then reached for her phone.

I assumed she'd Google it.

But no.

She opened ChatGPT.

That moment hit me hard.

I grew up in a world where "Just Google it" was the norm. But for her? Google wasn't the default.

She didn't want links—she wanted answers.

👉 No sifting through ten blue links.
👉 No ads disguised as content.
👉 No SEO-optimized fluff.

Just direct, distilled knowledge.

This is the future of search.

Google still dominates. But behavior is shifting. People don't just want information—they want insights.

So if you're still writing SEO content like it's 2015, here's a wake-up call:

🚫 AI will eat generic ToFu content alive.
✅ Deep expertise, real-world experience, and unique perspectives will win.

Search isn't dying. It's evolving.

Are you?

--- POST 2 ---
Instead of asking my daughter whether she wants to eat or not, I ask her if she wants to eat idly or dosa?
I get a positive response!

In the critically acclaimed movie, Lucifer, Mohanlal's character says:
"The battle is not between good and evil.
It is between (lesser) evil and (bigger) evil!"

Robert Greene, author of 48 Laws of Power, recommends:
"Give people options. Force them to make choices between the lesser of two evils, both of which serve your purpose."

My daughter chose to eat idly.
That made me a happy father!

Happy Father's Day!

--- POST 3 ---
My daughter is addicted to YouTube shorts.

It was getting out of hand:
🔶 She barely socializes,
🔶 She eats less, and
🔶 She sleeps late.

But then, everything changed when we brought home Tommy, a playful 25-day-old Shih Tzu!

He's now:
🍎 the apple of her eye,
🍎 her best friend, and
🍎 her new little sibling to care for.

She's loving every moment with him - playing, bathing, feeding, and even cleaning up after him (yes, you read that right!).

I'm thrilled, my mom's thrilled, and I'm sure Tommy is lapping up all the attention too!

Do you have a furry friend at home?

Do you think pets can be a positive distraction for kids, helping them break away from harmful digital habits?
